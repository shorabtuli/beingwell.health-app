Arthritis Clothing Adaptations: Dressing Comfortably with RA

By Being Well Team January 15, 2024

Discover practical clothing adaptations for arthritis patients. Learn about adaptive clothing, easy-fastening options, and style tips to make dressing easier and more comfortable.

Why Clothing Adaptations Matter

Arthritis can make everyday tasks like getting dressed challenging. Joint pain, stiffness, and reduced dexterity can turn simple activities into difficult ordeals. Adaptive clothing can help maintain independence and dignity while reducing frustration and pain.

Key Features of Adaptive Clothing

  • Easy Fastenings: Velcro, magnetic closures, or large buttons
  • Stretch Fabrics: Comfortable, flexible materials that accommodate swollen joints
  • Side or Back Openings: Alternative entry points for easier dressing
  • Adjustable Features: Elastic waistbands, adjustable straps, or drawstrings
  • Soft Materials: Gentle fabrics that don't irritate sensitive skin

Adaptive Clothing Options

Shirts and Tops

Look for shirts with front openings, magnetic closures, or Velcro fastenings. Button-down shirts with larger buttons or magnetic button replacements can make dressing easier. Consider tops with side openings or wrap-around styles.

Pants and Bottoms

Elastic waistbands, drawstring pants, or pants with Velcro closures are excellent options. Look for pants with side openings or that can be pulled on without fasteners. Consider adaptive jeans with magnetic fly closures.

Shoes and Footwear

Slip-on shoes, shoes with Velcro straps, or magnetic closures can make footwear easier to manage. Look for shoes with wide openings and soft, flexible materials that accommodate foot swelling.

DIY Adaptations

You can modify existing clothing to make it more arthritis-friendly:

  • Replace small buttons with larger ones or magnetic buttons
  • Add Velcro closures to replace zippers or buttons
  • Use button extenders to make buttoning easier
  • Add elastic to waistbands for easier fitting

Dressing Aids and Tools

Various tools can help with dressing:

  • Button Hooks: Help with buttoning shirts and pants
  • Zipper Pulls: Make zippers easier to grasp and pull
  • Sock Aids: Help put on socks without bending
  • Dressing Sticks: Assist with pulling on clothes
  • Long-Handled Shoe Horns: Help with putting on shoes

Style and Fashion Considerations

Adaptive clothing doesn't mean sacrificing style. Many companies now offer fashionable adaptive clothing that looks great while being functional. Look for:

  • Modern, stylish designs
  • Quality materials and construction
  • Versatile pieces that work for various occasions
  • Colors and patterns that suit your personal style

Shopping Tips

When shopping for adaptive clothing:

  • Try items on to ensure they're comfortable and easy to manage
  • Look for adjustable features that can accommodate changing symptoms
  • Consider the season and climate when choosing materials
  • Read reviews from other arthritis patients
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help or modifications

Conclusion

Adaptive clothing can significantly improve your daily life with arthritis. By choosing the right clothing and using helpful tools, you can maintain independence and comfort while looking great. Don't hesitate to explore adaptive options and make modifications that work for your specific needs.
