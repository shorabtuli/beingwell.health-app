Active Lifestyle with Arthritis: Staying Mobile and Healthy

By Being Well Team January 15, 2024

Discover how to maintain an active lifestyle while managing arthritis. Learn practical strategies for staying mobile, managing pain, and living well with RA.

Why Activity Matters for Arthritis

Staying active is crucial for managing arthritis symptoms. Regular movement helps maintain joint flexibility, strengthens supporting muscles, and can reduce pain and stiffness. An active lifestyle also improves overall health and mental well-being.

Safe Exercise Guidelines

  • Start Slowly: Begin with gentle activities and gradually increase intensity
  • Listen to Your Body: Stop if you experience increased pain or swelling
  • Warm Up: Always prepare your joints with gentle stretching
  • Stay Consistent: Regular, moderate activity is better than occasional intense exercise

Recommended Activities

Low-Impact Exercises

Walking, swimming, and cycling are excellent choices for people with arthritis. These activities provide cardiovascular benefits without putting excessive stress on joints.

Strength Training

Gentle strength training helps build muscle around affected joints, providing better support and stability. Use light weights or resistance bands and focus on proper form.

Flexibility Work

Stretching and yoga can improve range of motion and reduce stiffness. Focus on gentle movements and avoid positions that cause pain.

Daily Activity Tips

Incorporate movement into your daily routine. Take short walks, use the stairs when possible, and find ways to stay active throughout the day. Even small amounts of activity can make a significant difference.

Managing Pain During Activity

Use heat or cold therapy before and after exercise. Consider taking pain medication as prescribed by your doctor. Remember that some discomfort is normal, but sharp or worsening pain should be addressed.

Building a Support System

Consider joining exercise classes designed for people with arthritis or working with a physical therapist. Having support and guidance can help you stay motivated and exercise safely.

Conclusion
