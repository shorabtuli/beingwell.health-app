Workplace Accommodations for RA - Expert-Recommended Strategies

Your rights, resources, and strategies for creating a supportive work environment

10 min read January 15, 2024 Being Well Team

Living with rheumatoid arthritis doesn't mean you have to give up your career or professional goals. With the right accommodations and support, you can thrive in the workplace while managing your RA symptoms. Understanding your rights and knowing how to request accommodations is crucial for maintaining your health and career success.

In the United States, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) protects employees with disabilities, including rheumatoid arthritis, from discrimination in the workplace. This law requires employers to provide reasonable accommodations that allow you to perform your job duties effectively.

Flexible Work Arrangements

Flexibility in work arrangements can significantly improve your ability to manage RA symptoms while maintaining productivity and job satisfaction.

  • Flexible Hours: Adjusting start and end times to accommodate morning stiffness or fatigue
  • Remote Work: Working from home on days when symptoms are more severe
  • Part-time Options: Reducing hours while maintaining benefits
  • Job Sharing: Splitting responsibilities with another employee
  • Compressed Workweeks: Working longer days but fewer days per week

How to Request Accommodations

Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Document Your Needs

Keep detailed records of how RA affects your work performance. Note specific challenges, symptoms, and potential solutions that could help.

Step 2: Research Your Rights

Familiarize yourself with ADA requirements, your company's accommodation policies, and any relevant state laws that might apply.

Step 3: Prepare Your Request

Write a formal accommodation request letter. Be specific about your needs and how the requested accommodations will help you perform your job effectively.

Step 4: Submit Your Request

Submit your request to the appropriate person (usually HR or your supervisor). Keep copies of all correspondence and documentation.

Step 5: Follow Up

Stay in communication with your employer about the status of your request. Be prepared to provide additional documentation if needed.
