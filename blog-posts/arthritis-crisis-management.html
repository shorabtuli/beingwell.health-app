Arthritis Crisis Management: Emergency Care for RA Flares

By Being Well Team January 15, 2024

Essential strategies for managing arthritis crises and RA flares. Learn when to seek emergency care, how to provide immediate relief, and develop a crisis management plan.

Recognizing an Arthritis Crisis

An arthritis crisis or severe flare can develop suddenly and require immediate attention. Understanding the signs and having a plan in place can help you manage these situations effectively and prevent complications.

Warning Signs of a Crisis

  • Severe Pain: Pain that is significantly worse than usual and doesn't respond to normal treatments
  • Joint Swelling: Rapid or severe swelling that limits movement
  • Fever: Elevated temperature that may indicate infection
  • Fatigue: Extreme tiredness that interferes with daily activities
  • Loss of Function: Inability to perform basic tasks or move affected joints

Immediate Crisis Management

1. Assess the Situation

Determine the severity of your symptoms. If you're experiencing extreme pain, severe swelling, or other concerning symptoms, don't hesitate to seek medical attention.

2. Pain Relief Strategies

Apply ice or heat as appropriate, take prescribed medications, and rest the affected joints. Avoid activities that worsen your symptoms.

3. Contact Your Healthcare Team

Reach out to your rheumatologist or primary care provider. They can provide guidance on managing the crisis and may need to adjust your treatment plan.

When to Seek Emergency Care

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:

  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Severe, unrelenting pain
  • Signs of infection (fever, redness, warmth around joints)
  • Sudden loss of function in multiple joints
  • Severe fatigue or weakness

Preparing for Crises

Develop a crisis management plan with your healthcare team. This should include:

  • Emergency contact numbers
  • List of current medications
  • Instructions for managing flares
  • Plan for when to seek medical attention

Prevention Strategies

While not all crises can be prevented, maintaining good disease management can reduce their frequency and severity. Follow your treatment plan, manage stress, and maintain a healthy lifestyle.

Support During Crises

Don't hesitate to ask for help from family, friends, or support groups. Having a support system in place can make managing crises much easier and less stressful.
