üí° Important Note

Start with the MOST IMPORTANT pillars first: Nutrition, Hydration, and Sleep. These three form the foundation of your RA management. The other pillars can be added gradually as you build your routine. Remember, you didn't develop RA overnight, and building these habits takes time - be patient with yourself.

ü•ó 1. Nutrition - The Foundation

Start here: Avoid inflammatory foods, particularly gluten and dairy. These are the most common triggers for RA inflammation.

‚úÖ Foods to Focus On:

  • Fresh vegetables: Broccoli, cauliflower, spinach, brussels sprouts, asparagus
  • Fruits: Any fruits you enjoy (berries, apples, oranges, etc.)
  • Lean proteins: Chicken, tofu, edamame
  • Salads: With low-fat, gluten-free, dairy-free dressings

‚ùå Foods to Avoid:

  • Deep fried foods
  • Processed foods (in cans or bags)
  • Fast food
  • Gluten and dairy products

üìö Raw Diet Option:

There's a book called "Medical Medium" that discusses a raw diet approach. While the author's theories about doctors and disease origins may be controversial, many people (including myself) have found the raw diet suggestions helpful for RA. Important: Over time, you'll need to add protein and calcium to your diet.

üíß 2. Hydration - The Essential

Target: 100 ounces of water daily

üíß Hydration Strategy:

  • Start your day: 3-4 large glasses of water first thing in the morning
  • Consistent schedule: Drink water throughout the day on a regular schedule
  • Sleep protection: Avoid drinking water after 7 PM to prevent sleep disruption

Add this to my daily routine ‚Üí

üò¥ 3. Sleep - The Recovery Pillar

Your body needs quality sleep to fight inflammation. This is crucial for RA management.

üò¥ Sleep Optimization:

  • Consistent schedule: Go to sleep and wake up at the same time every day
  • Pre-sleep routine: Avoid eating or drinking too much before bedtime
  • Sleep environment: Use white noise, cooler room temperature, comfortable clothes
  • Darkness: Make the room as dark as possible
  • Calming aids: Try CBD oil/gummies or breathing exercises if needed
  • Reading: Read a physical book (not Kindle or phone) to calm down

üí¨ 4. Community - The Support Network

Find people you can talk to every day. This is crucial for emotional support and maintaining positivity.

üí¨ Community Building:

  • Daily connection: Talk to someone every day about your experiences
  • Emotional outlet: Have people to complain to when you're not feeling well
  • Positive distraction: Discuss other topics to take your mind off the disease
  • Arthritis.org: Join their periodic Zoom meetings for RA patients
  • Find joy: Spend time with babies - they provide pure happiness and distraction

üíÜ 5. Self-Care - The Mental Health Pillar

Be kind to yourself and find activities that bring you joy and mental relief.

üíÜ Self-Care Activities:

  • Read a book
  • Watch TV shows or movies
  • Eat enjoyable food (avoiding gluten/dairy)
  • Talk to friends
  • Play games
  • Do anything that gives your mind a break from RA

Add this to my daily routine ‚Üí

üßò 6. Stretching & Exercise - The Movement Pillar

Strengthen joints and muscles to better handle daily stresses and reduce flare-ups.

üßò Exercise Strategy:

  • Start small: Begin with 1 rep of stretching and 1 rep of exercise
  • Gradual increase: Add 1 rep each day (2 reps tomorrow, 3 the next day)
  • Resistance bands: Use for biceps and other exercises
  • Joint positioning: Be mindful of how you position joints during daily activities
  • Example: When cooking, use both hands under pots to distribute weight

üì∫ Recommended Resources:

Check out these YouTube channels for exercises and stretches:

  • Tone and Tighten
  • Resistance Band Exercises

Search YouTube for "stretching and exercise with resistance bands" for more examples.

üß† 7. Meditation & Journaling - The Mindfulness Pillar

Keep yourself positive and mentally strong through mindfulness practices.

üß† Mindfulness Practices:

  • Meditation: Start with 5-minute YouTube meditation videos
  • Gratitude journal: Write down 3 things you're grateful for each day
  • Timing: Practice in the morning or evening, whichever works best for you

üíä 8. Medicine - The Medical Pillar

Stay diligent about your medications and medical treatment plan.

üíä Medication Management:

  • Take medications as prescribed
  • Set up reminders if needed
  • Track any side effects
  • Communicate with your healthcare team
